## 1. Five Headline Options (ranked strongest first)

1. **"Get your consultants 2,000 billable hours back this year. Without hiring, without enterprise AI, without changing how your team works."**
   - Why strongest: Specific number + time period + addresses three failed alternatives in one line. Removes the "but I'd have to change everything" objection before it forms.

2. **"Your GDPR consultants answer the same questions every week. This system finds the cited paragraph in 8 seconds."**
   - Pattern interrupt + specificity. The "same questions every week" is a visceral recognition moment for the buyer.

3. **"Stop paying senior consultants to scroll through 600-page PDFs. Deploy cited research in 30 days."**
   - Direct cost framing + timeline. "Scroll through 600-page PDFs" is the most visual pain image.

4. **"A research system that knows your regulatory corpus, your templates, and your firm's vocabulary. Deployed in 30 days, owned by you."**
   - Ownership angle leads. For buyers burned by vendor lock-in, this hits first.

5. **"2,000 hours of ctrl+F per year. One system to replace it. 30 days to deploy. You own the code."**
   - Staccato rhythm. Works well as a bold typographic treatment. Less emotional, more rational.

## 3. Conversion Infrastructure Spec

### Google Analytics 4

Track these events: `cta_click` (with parameters: button_location [hero, mid-page, final], button_text), `scroll_depth` (fire at 25%, 50%, 75%, 100% thresholds), `time_on_page` (fire at 30s, 60s, 120s, 300s intervals), `form_submission` (Calendly embed interaction, tracked via postMessage listener on Calendly's scheduling confirmation event), `video_play` (if hero video is implemented, track play/pause/completion), `faq_expand` (track which FAQ questions get opened, indicates which objections are live). Set up a custom dimension for `landing_page_variant` to segment by A/B test group. Funnel: page_view → scroll_50 → cta_click → form_submission.

---

### Microsoft Clarity

Enable heatmaps and session recordings on the /compliance route. Configure two custom tags: `high_value_zone_1` for the above-the-fold section (viewport 0-100vh) and `high_value_zone_2` for the pricing comparison section. Set up a smart event for "rage clicks" (repeated clicks on non-interactive elements, which indicates confusion). Filter session recordings to show only sessions where the user scrolled past 50% but did NOT click a CTA. These are the "almost converted" sessions. Review 10 per week for the first month to identify where attention drops off or confusion appears.

### A/B Test Plan (priority order)

**Test 1: Headline variants.** Test headline #1 ("Get your consultants 2,000 billable hours back...") against headline #2 ("Your GDPR consultants answer the same questions every week..."). Primary metric: scroll-to-50% rate (indicates the headline held attention). Secondary: CTA click rate. Run until 200 sessions per variant minimum.

**Test 2: Hero visual.** Test the 8-second screen recording loop against the static split-screen mockup (14-min scroll vs. 8-sec result). Primary metric: time-on-page above 60 seconds. Secondary: first CTA click rate. This test answers whether the motion of the video loop creates engagement or whether the static contrast image communicates value faster.

**Test 3: Above-the-fold CTA copy.** Test "Book Your 15-Minute Strategy Call" against "See It Running on Your Documents." Primary metric: CTA click-through rate on the hero button specifically. The second variant emphasizes the Diagnostic Demo (low commitment) over the call (higher commitment). This tells us whether the ICP prefers a "see proof first" path or a "talk to the builder" path.

### Userbrain Test Brief

Recruit 5 testers who match the ICP: managing partner, senior consultant, or operations lead at a GDPR/privacy consultancy with 10-80 staff in Western Europe. Give them the page URL and these five tasks/questions:

1. "Without scrolling, what does this company do and who is it for? Tell me in your own words."
   (Tests: above-the-fold clarity. Do they get it in 5 seconds?)

   (Tests: which sections create engagement vs. friction. Captures the natural reading path.)

3. "Imagine your firm is considering this. What would your managing partner's first objection be? Now find where the page addresses it."
   (Tests: whether objection handling is findable and convincing.)

4. "Look at the pricing comparison. Does the cost make sense to you? What questions do you still have about what you'd be paying for?"

5. "If you were going to take the next step, what would you do? How confident are you that this would be worth your time?"
   (Tests: CTA clarity and trust level. Captures final conversion friction.)

## 4. What I'd Test First

**Headline variant test, run immediately.** The headline determines whether 60% of visitors stay or bounce. Everything below it is irrelevant if the above-the-fold fails. Test headline #1 (specific number + emotional payoff) against headline #2 (pattern-interrupt recognition) for two weeks or 200 sessions per variant, whichever comes first. Measure scroll-to-50% as the primary metric because CTA clicks will be too sparse at early traffic volumes to reach significance. Once the winning headline is locked, move to hero visual testing.

Secondary recommendation: before running paid A/B tests, do the Userbrain qualitative test first. Five sessions at €50/tester = €250 total, and you'll likely identify one major friction point (confusing section, misunderstood pricing, unclear CTA path) that no quantitative test would surface. Fix that first, then run the headline split.
